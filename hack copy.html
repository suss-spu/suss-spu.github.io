Hack the next chapter of learning.

The SUSS Future Learning Hackathon is a campus-based innovation sprint organised by Singapore University of Social Sciences. It brings together students, educators, builders, and industry partners to prototype practical ideas for learning, teaching, education use cases, and agentic AI.

This is not a generic coding event. The challenge focus is clear: build tools, workflows, and experiences that make education more human, more effective, and more scalable for the future of teaching and lifelong learning.

Challenge Themes

Proposed tracks for learning, teaching, and agentic AI.

These themes are designed to attract strong student teams while keeping the work anchored to real education needs. Each track can be supported by sponsoring partners through problem statements, APIs, datasets, mentors, judges, or pilot pathways.

01
AI for Teaching Design

Lesson planning, rubric generation, activity design, feedback support, and tools that help educators teach with more clarity and less repetitive workload.

02
Agentic AI for Student Support

Goal-setting agents, study coaches, onboarding assistants, advising workflows, and support systems that guide students across the learning journey.

03
Responsible AI in Assessment

Formative feedback, reflective learning, authentic assessment design, academic integrity support, and transparent AI-assisted evaluation experiences.

04
Education Use Cases for Lifelong Learning

Skills mapping, modular learning, workplace learning pathways, adult learner support, and systems that connect education to employability and growth.

05
Inclusive and Accessible Learning

Multilingual learning experiences, accessibility-first design, assistive technology, neuroinclusive tools, and equitable digital learning support.

06
Learning Analytics and Early Intervention

Insight dashboards, risk detection, timely nudges, and decision support that help educators identify opportunities to improve engagement and outcomes.

Call for Partners

Invitation to sponsoring partners

SUSS welcomes sponsoring partners who want to help shape the future of education. We are looking for organisations that care about learning innovation, digital transformation, student success, responsible AI, and applied experimentation in real-world teaching contexts.

Partner with SUSS to turn ideas into usable education solutions.

Benefits

Clear value for sponsors and students

The event is designed to create practical value on both sides: sponsors gain access to new ideas, talent, and emerging use cases, while students gain experience, mentorship, and real exposure to innovation in education.

Benefits for sponsors
  • Access high-potential student talent with strong interest in AI and education.
  • Increase brand visibility within the SUSS community and wider innovation ecosystem.
  • Test products, platforms, or APIs against relevant education use cases.
  • Discover fresh prototype ideas tied to real learning and teaching challenges.
  • Engage educators and academic stakeholders in a hands-on innovation setting.
  • Spot pilot opportunities, internship candidates, and future collaborators early.
Benefits for students
  • Work on meaningful problems that connect AI innovation with real education needs.
  • Learn how to build practical solutions for learning, teaching, and student support.
  • Receive mentorship from SUSS faculty, industry experts, and sponsoring partners.
  • Strengthen portfolios with prototypes, presentations, and teamwork experience.
  • Gain exposure to internships, pilot projects, and career conversations with sponsors.
  • Collaborate across disciplines and build confidence in turning ideas into outcomes.
Venue

Hosted at SUSS, an autonomous university in Singapore

Holding the hackathon at Singapore University of Social Sciences gives the event a credible education setting. It places teams in a campus environment where teaching, learner support, and lifelong learning are already central to the institution's mission.

Venue highlights

  • Suitable spaces for workshops, team collaboration, mentoring, and final demos.
  • Direct relevance for education-focused prototypes and stakeholder conversations.
  • Strong positioning for sponsors that want visibility in a credible academic environment.
