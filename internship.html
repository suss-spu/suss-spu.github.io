Why this matters

Good for interns. Good for the community.

The point is not to keep interns busy. It is to help them grow through useful work, while giving the community better tools, ideas, and experiments.

What interns gain

Stronger judgment, sharper execution, and work they can stand behind. The learning comes from shipping, not watching.

What the community gains

Fast prototypes, practical research, and service improvements shaped around real needs instead of assumptions.

How it works

Short loop. Visible impact.

The internship stays practical: start with a real problem, make something tangible, learn from feedback, and leave behind work others can build on.

  1. 01 OBSERVE Start from a real friction point.
  2. 02 BUILD Turn insight into something people can test.
  3. 03 SHARE Document what worked so it lasts.

Make work that teaches you and helps someone else.
